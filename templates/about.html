About This Tool

FelineGenetics is a Bio595 course project by Xiaoyue and Dekun. It helps cat owners, breeders, and vets understand a cat's genetic health risks from a standard VCF (Variant Call Format) file produced by most pet DNA labs.

How the Genetics Module Works

  1. You upload a .vcf file — this is the raw DNA output from sequencing labs.
  2. Our parser reads every variant line by line.
  3. Each variant is matched against our local SQLite database of known feline health variants, sourced from OMIA and published research.
  4. The genotype (one copy vs two copies) is combined with the inheritance pattern to decide if your cat is a carrier or is affected.
  5. Results are grouped by severity (High / Medium / Low) and written in plain English.

Data Sources

  • OMIA — Online Mendelian Inheritance in Animals: the primary catalog of known genetic variants in domestic animals.
  • Peer-reviewed feline genetics literature (severity levels and breed risks added by hand).

Limitations

  • Our database covers only well-studied, published variants. Many VCF lines will not match — this is normal and does not mean the cat is guaranteed healthy.
  • This tool is for educational awareness only. It is not a substitute for veterinary diagnosis.
  • VCF files from different labs use slightly different formats. If your file is not parsed correctly, please contact us.
